
Google Fonts has an amazing, free selection. Here are the best English fonts, categorized by where you should use them in your UI:

### 1. Headings & Gamification (For Level names, Ranks, and Big Titles)
These fonts are thick, grab attention instantly, and give a video game feel:
* **Fredoka (or Fredoka One):** A thick, playful font with rounded edges. It's absolutely perfect for writing things like (Level 1, Bronze Novice) or large section titles like (Cyber Quests).
* **Poetsen One:** Looks like action-packed gaming banners. Great for large buttons or winning notifications (XP Gained!).

### 2. UI & Body Text (For Menus, Buttons, and Daily Tasks)
Here we need fonts that are very easy on the eyes, highly readable, yet "informal":
* **Nunito:** The top and most popular choice for this purpose. It is a rounded, comfortable font that looks professional yet very friendly. Perfect for all the general text in the app.
* **Quicksand:** A geometric font with rounded terminals. It gives a simplified, modern "tech-lite" vibe and would look fantastic in the Main Menu and Daily Tasks.
* **Varela Round:** Soft and simple, very similar to the fonts used in popular language-learning apps or interactive games.

### 3. AI Mascot Speech Bubbles
To distinguish the AI assistant's voice from the rest of the app and make it feel like a friend talking:
* **Balsamiq Sans** or **Comic Neue:** These fonts look like neat handwriting or comic books. Using them in the robot's speech bubbles will make its security tips feel like a fun story rather than a school lesson.

**Pro-Tip for Font Pairing:** The best approach in UI design is to combine two fonts. For example: Use **Fredoka** for big headings and numbers (like the 23 Security Score), and use **Nunito** or **Quicksand** for the rest of the smaller text and menus.
