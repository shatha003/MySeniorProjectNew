# Chapter 5: Results and Discussion

The main results of your work should be presented here, together with critical discussion. The chapter should cover three things (although these would not be used as section headings):

5.1 Findings - present all the results (products, experimental findings, theories, etc.) generated during the project.
- This may also include some off-topic findings that were not expected, or which were side-effects of other explorations.

5.2 Goals achieved - describes the degree to which the findings support the original objectives laid out for the project.
- The goals may be partially or fully achieved, or exceeded.
- Note that reporting of failures to achieve goals is important since a fundamental feature of the assessment procedures is that the processes (how you went about your project) are often as important as the products of the project.

5.3 Further work - describes two things:
- Firstly, new areas of investigation prompted by developments in this project,
- Secondly parts of the current work which were not completed due to time constraints and/or problems encountered.

## Report Requirements

Your report must include, but not be limited to, the following:

- Problem definition and a Clear statement of aims, scope, deliverables, and the target customer.
- Provide a clear, logical, and comprehensive suggested solution that involves disabilities and different social backgrounds. Applying an approach to the solution of complex problems.
- A good and clear review of relevant work by other authors (at least 2000 words).
- Comparing and contrasting with other works.
- Functional and Non-functional Requirements.
- Research methodology (for NSP).
- The list of hardware components and/or software tools that were used in the project, and the role of each hardware component used in the system (for a hardware project).
- Providing and explaining the specification model (Data Flow Diagram, Use case, component diagram (for NSP), etc.). Your model should identify and refine the content of the project.
- Provide clear evidence for the consideration of social, professional, legal, and ethical issues in relation to the project.
- Proper use of citations and references (journals, proceedings of international conferences, books, and others).
- Spelling and grammar.
- Style (font size and type, spacing, etc.).
